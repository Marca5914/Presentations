Building a Search: Two Types of Terms

Controlled Vocabulary (MeSH)
Keywords

Controlled Vocabulary (MeSH)

Medical Subject Headings are the database's "official" subject headings. Finds articles about a topic. (e.g., "Myocardial Infarction")

Keywords

The author's own words. Finds articles that mention a term. Essential for new topics. (e.g., "heart attack")

An expert search uses BOTH.

The Language of Search: Boolean Logic & More

Concept 1 OR Synonym 1 → Broadens your search
Concept A AND Concept B → Narrows your search

(elderly OR aged) AND (exercise OR "physical activity")

( ) Use Parentheses to group OR statements

" " Use Quotation Marks for exact phrases

* Use Truncation for word variations (e.g., therap*)

Refining Your Search: Filters & NOT

Use Filters to Focus

After your initial search, use the database's built-in filters to narrow the results:

  • Date of Publication: Limit to the last 5 or 10 years.
  • Article Type: Focus on `Systematic Review` or `Randomized Controlled Trial`.
  • Language: Select English or other relevant languages.

The NOT Operator: Use with Caution!

`NOT` excludes terms, but can easily remove relevant results.

Example: `Hypertension NOT Pregnancy`

This removes studies on pregnant women, but might also remove a key review that has a single chapter on non-pregnant hypertension.

Best practice: Avoid `NOT`. Use filters instead.

The AI Revolution in Research

Why AI? To manage information overload and accelerate discovery.

Benefits: Speed, finding novel connections, summarizing complex text.

CRITICAL Cautions

  • Hallucinations: AI can invent fake citations.
  • Bias: AI can inherit and amplify bias from its training data.
  • The Paywall Problem: Most AIs can't read articles behind paywalls.

VERIFY EVERYTHING.

AI in Practice: Meet the Tools

LLMs (ChatGPT/Gemini)

Describe your research idea in plain English. These tools can help you refine it into a structured question and suggest relevant keywords.

Great for brainstorming & question refinement.

NotebookLM

Upload your final papers to create a private knowledge base. Ask questions, get summaries, and find connections across your sources.

Excellent for analysis and synthesis.

A Modern, Hybrid Workflow

1

Brainstorm (AI)

Use LLMs to refine your question and find keywords.

2

Search (Traditional)

Build a rigorous, documented search in PubMed & Embase.

3

Analyze (AI)

Upload sources to NotebookLM to ask questions and synthesize findings.

4

Synthesize (AI-Assisted)

Use AI to help draft, but ALWAYS verify and rewrite.

Key Takeaways

1. Start with PICO: A clear question is your most important tool.
2. Be Systematic: Use multiple databases and combine MeSH with keywords.
3. Use AI as an Accelerator, Not a Replacement.
4. Trust, but Verify: Critically evaluate all AI-generated output. You are the expert in the loop.
